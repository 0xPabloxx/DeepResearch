Mykola Dmytrovych Leontovych was born on January 20, 1877, in the village of Verkhnie Subchy, located in the Kharkiv Governorate of the Russian Empire (now part of Ukraine). His contributions to Ukrainian music are significant, and his legacy includes not just "Shchedryk" but also various other choral compositions that remain central to Eastern European musical traditions.

Leontovych’s life was marked by dedication to music education and composition. He studied at Kyiv University and taught music throughout his career. Despite facing political turmoil during his lifetime, including persecution under Soviet authorities, his works continue to be celebrated for their innovation and cultural resonance.

## The Origin of “Carol of the Bells”

“Shchedryk,” the original Ukrainian song composed by Leontovych, became well-known after performances by the Kyiv Academic Capella choir in 1916. It features a melody that mimics the song of a swan goose, symbolizing prosperity and renewal. In the 1930s, the piece was translated into English and adapted into the now-familiar Christmas carol "Carol of the Bells," whose lyrics do not reference Christmas directly but evoke the spirit of winter and celebration.

## Legacy

Many institutions, festivals, and choirs worldwide continue to honor his memory and celebrate his music.
