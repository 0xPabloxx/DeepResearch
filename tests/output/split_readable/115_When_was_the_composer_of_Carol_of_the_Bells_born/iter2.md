## Mykola Leontovych: Background and Life

Mykola Leontovych was born on January 28, 1877. His birthplace was the village of Vesyno, located in the Poltava Governorate of the Russian Empire (now part of Ukraine). Leontovych's upbringing in rural Ukraine deeply influenced his musical style, drawing heavily on Ukrainian folk traditions and melodies.

He showed musical talent from an early age and pursued formal music education. Eventually, he became a teacher, conductor, and composer, dedicating much of his career to promoting Ukrainian culture and music, especially during times when national identity was being suppressed under imperial rule.

## The Creation of "Carol of the Bells"

Leontovych's arrangement of "Shchedryk"—which later became known in the West as "Carol of the Bells"—was created in 1916. Originally, "Shchedryk" was a Ukrainian spring song celebrating a swallow entering a house, symbolizing prosperity and good fortune. Leontovych transformed this into a festive choral work with four distinct parts, characterized by its haunting melody and rhythmic ostinato, making it memorable and impactful.

In 1936, American composer Peter J. Wilhousky adapted the melody into English-language lyrics, giving birth to the internationally recognized Christmas carol "Carol of the Bells." While Wilhousky played a significant role in popularizing the tune across the globe, the original compositional credit belongs to Leontovych.
