# Question

When did the president who warned about the military industrial complex die?

# Ground Truth Answer

March 28, 1969

# Model Prediction


The phrase "military-industrial complex" is most famously associated with President Dwight D. Eisenhower, who warned about its growing influence in American society during his farewell address in January 1961. This warning was a pivotal moment in American political history, highlighting concerns about the relationship between national defense spending, the government, and private industry.

Dwight D. Eisenhower served as the 34th President of the United States from 1953 to 1961. His administration oversaw significant events such as the end of the Korean War and the early stages of the Cold War. Despite his military background and support for national defense, Eisenhower expressed caution regarding the potential dangers posed by the entanglement of defense interests and industrial corporations. He feared that this "complex" might acquire unwarranted influence over national policy, especially during peacetime.

After leaving office, Eisenhower remained active in public life but struggled with declining health. His presidency had been marked by both major successes and some controversies, including the Atoms for Peace initiative, interstate highway construction, and the space race response to the Soviet Union’s Sputnik launch. Still, it was his warning about the military-industrial complex that stands as one of his most enduring legacies.

President Eisenhower passed away on March 28, 1969. He was 78 years old at the time of his death. His passing came after several years of health problems, including heart disease and previous surgery. Eisenhower spent his final days at the Walter Reed Army Medical Center in Washington, D.C.

Eisenhower’s warning about the military-industrial complex has continued to resonate in American politics and public discourse. Many historians, political analysts, and commentators reference his words when discussing topics such as defense budgets, lobbying, the role of large contractors, and the impact of military priorities on domestic policy. The speech remains a critical text for understanding how leaders balance national security needs with democratic values and accountability.

In summary, the president who famously warned about the military-industrial complex was Dwight D. Eisenhower. He died on March 28, 1969, after a long career dedicated to public service and leadership during one of the most transformative periods in American history.
