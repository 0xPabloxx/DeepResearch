## The Yangtze River: Geography and Significance

The Yangtze River originates at the Tanggula Mountains in Tibet, China, and flows eastward across China until it empties into the East China Sea. With a length of approximately 6,300 kilometers, it is the longest river in Asia and the third-longest in the world.

**Geographic Path:**
- Starts in Tibet (Chinese region)
- Flows through multiple Chinese provinces including Qinghai, Sichuan, Hubei, Hunan, Jiangxi, Anhui, Jiangsu, and Shanghai

No part of the Yangtze River crosses international borders; its entire course remains within the People's Republic of China.

The Yangtze’s significance is not just in discharge but also in its role in transportation, irrigation, hydroelectric power (notably with the Three Gorges Dam), and as a cultural icon in China.

## The Yangtze River: Geography and Significance

The Yangtze River originates at the Tanggula Mountains in Tibet, China, and flows eastward across China until it empties into the East China Sea. With a length of approximately 6,300 kilometers, it is the longest river in Asia and the third-longest in the world.

**Geographic Path:**
- Starts in Tibet (Chinese region)
- Flows through multiple Chinese provinces including Qinghai, Sichuan, Hubei, Hunan, Jiangxi, Anhui, Jiangsu, and Shanghai

No part of the Yangtze River crosses international borders; its entire course remains within the People's Republic of China.

## Context: Why Is the Yangtze Considered Third?

While both the Congo and the Amazon far surpass the Yangtze in discharge, the latter consistently ranks third among single-river systems. Some river systems, notably the Ganges–Brahmaputra–Meghna, can have substantial combined discharge and traverse several countries. However, these are often treated as separate entries or as a collective system, and their individual average discharges are generally lower than the Yangtze’s.

The Yangtze’s significance is not just in discharge but also in its role in transportation, irrigation, hydroelectric power (notably with the Three Gorges Dam), and as a cultural icon in China.
