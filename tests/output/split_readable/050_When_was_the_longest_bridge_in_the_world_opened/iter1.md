The longest bridge in the world, in terms of total length, is the Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge located in China. Its primary purpose is to carry high-speed trains between major cities, allowing for efficient transportation across complex terrain, including numerous rivers and lakes.

The Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge was officially opened and put into service on June 30, 2011. This date marks the commencement of operations for the entire Beijing–Shanghai High-Speed Railway, of which the bridge forms a crucial segment. The completion of the bridge itself was achieved earlier—in 2010—but it became operational for railway traffic on June 30, 2011, coinciding with the launch of regular high-speed train services along the line.

## Significance of the Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge

The bridge holds the title of the world's longest bridge due to its vast total length, far exceeding that of other notable bridges around the globe. Its construction required advanced engineering techniques and substantial investment, given the challenges posed by the geography of eastern China, where numerous water bodies and marshlands needed to be traversed.

The bridge consists largely of a viaduct structure, featuring dozens of piers supported on drilled shaft foundations. By elevating the tracks above ground level, the Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge minimizes disruption to existing land use and avoids flooding risks common in low-lying areas. It exemplifies the scale and ambition of China’s infrastructure development in the 21st century.

## Historical Context and Comparison

When discussing “the longest bridge,” it is important to clarify whether one refers to total length or just the main span. Some people might confuse “longest bridge” with “longest main span”—a different criterion often used for arch, suspension, and cable-stayed bridges. Under the main span definition, the Akashi Kaikyō Bridge in Japan takes the record, having opened on April 5, 1998, and featuring a central span of 1,991 meters. However, if referring to overall bridge length—which is the standard interpretation—the Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge is unmatched.
