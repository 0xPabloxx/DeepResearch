This remarkable structure is part of the Beijing–Shanghai High-Speed Railway and holds the record for its extraordinary length, stretching approximately 164.8 kilometers (about 102 miles).

The Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge officially opened on June 30, 2011. Its completion marked a significant milestone in global engineering and infrastructure development, symbolizing China’s rapid advancement in large-scale construction projects. The bridge began operations as an essential segment of the Beijing–Shanghai High-Speed Railway, facilitating faster and more efficient transportation between two of China’s most important cities.

**About the Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge**

The Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge is primarily a viaduct constructed over waterlogged areas, marshes, and numerous rivers in the Yangtze River Delta region. Its main function is to support high-speed trains traveling along the Beijing–Shanghai line, but its design and scale are also feats of modern civil engineering, overcoming significant geographical and environmental challenges.

Constructed starting in November 2008, the project involved vast amounts of labor, materials, and innovative construction techniques. The bridge consists of more than 4,700 piers and over 9,000 steel beams, reflecting both the technological sophistication and logistical planning required for such an undertaking. The bridge serves not only as a critical transportation corridor but also as an icon of the country’s infrastructure ambitions.

**Significance of the Opening Date**

The official opening of the Danyang–Kunshan Grand Bridge occurred on June 30, 2011. This date coincided with the inauguration of commercial operations for the Beijing–Shanghai High-Speed Railway. On this day, high-speed trains began regular service across the bridge, dramatically reducing travel time between Beijing and Shanghai from more than a day to just around five hours.

This achievement demonstrated China's capacity to deliver megaprojects at scale and highlighted advancements in rail technology, safety, and efficiency. The bridge’s opening was celebrated as a key event in China’s broader strategy to enhance regional connectivity and economic integration.

**Conclusion**
