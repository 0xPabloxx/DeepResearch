## Definitions of Bird Speed Measurements

Birds can reach their highest velocities in different circumstances:

- **Dive Speed (Stooping):** Birds of prey, especially raptors, often achieve their fastest speeds while diving at prey. The peregrine falcon is the most famous example, reaching speeds exceeding 320 km/h during steep dives.
- **Level Flight:** Other birds, especially swifts and swallows, reach their fastest speeds while flying horizontally in pursuit of insects or migrating long distances. These speeds are considerably lower than those achieved during dives.

The ranking of birds by speed differs depending on whether the measurement includes or excludes dive speeds.
