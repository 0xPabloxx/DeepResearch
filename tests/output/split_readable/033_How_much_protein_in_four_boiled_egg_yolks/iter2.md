## Nutritional Basics of Egg Yolks

Egg yolks are a significant source of high-quality protein and are packed with other essential nutrients like vitamins A, D, E, K, B vitamins, choline, iron, and zinc. While the egg white contains most of the protein found in an egg, the yolk still contributes importantly both in terms of protein and overall nutrient density.

The main questions are:
- How much protein is typically in one egg yolk?
- Does boiling affect the protein content?
- How does yolk size influence protein content?

## Protein Content Per Egg Yolk

On average, a large egg yolk weighs about 17–18 grams. According to widely used nutritional databases, such as those provided by food authorities and scientific organizations, each large egg yolk contains approximately 2.7 to 3.0 grams of protein. This figure comes from analysis of egg yolks per 100 grams, adjusted for the actual weight of a single yolk.

### Example Calculation (per yolk):

| Medium      | ~13                | 16.4                | ~2.1                 |
| Large       | ~18                | 16.4                | ~2.9                 |
| Extra-Large | ~20                | 16.4                | ~3.3                 |

*Note: The protein per 100g value is standard for raw egg yolks; protein content does not significantly change with boiling.*

## Total Protein in Four Boiled Egg Yolks

Based on the calculations above:
- **Small yolks**: 4 x 1.6 = 6.4 grams
- **Medium yolks**: 4 x 2.1 = 8.4 grams
- **Large yolks**: 4 x 2.9 = 11.6 grams
- **Extra-large yolks**: 4 x 3.3 = 13.2 grams

If you're using large egg yolks (the most common size for recipes and nutrition labels), you can expect each yolk to have about 2.9–3.0 grams of protein. Therefore, four large boiled egg yolks contain roughly **11.6–12 grams of protein**.

## Effect of Cooking Method

Boiling an egg does not change the protein content—it remains the same in cooked or raw yolks. However, since boiling removes some moisture from the yolk, the absolute protein mass per gram may appear slightly higher than in raw yolks. Practically, though, this difference is minimal, especially when measuring out whole yolks rather than weighing per gram.

## Variations and Practical Considerations

- The size of the egg will influence the total protein in the yolks consumed. If your eggs are consistently larger or smaller, weigh the yolks before cooking for maximum accuracy.
- If you are tracking daily protein intake precisely—such as for athletic performance or diet planning—consider weighing your yolks at home.
- Be aware that commercial or organic eggs may vary slightly in composition depending on the hen's diet and breed, but these differences are generally minor compared to the effect of size.

## Other Nutritional Highlights

Besides protein, egg yolks also provide:
- Healthy fats and cholesterol
- Fat-soluble vitamins (A, D, E, K)
- Water-soluble vitamins (especially B vitamins like folate and B12)
- Essential minerals like iron and phosphorus

This makes egg yolks a valuable part of many balanced diets.

## Conclusion

In summary, if you eat four large boiled egg yolks, you can expect to consume approximately **11–12 grams of protein**. The exact amount may fluctuate depending on the actual weight of the yolks, but this is a reliable estimate based on standard nutritional tables and scientific data.

For most people, this protein contribution is meaningful, especially when added to a meal that includes other protein-rich foods or eaten as a standalone snack. If you need more precise numbers for health reasons, consider weighing your eggs directly.
