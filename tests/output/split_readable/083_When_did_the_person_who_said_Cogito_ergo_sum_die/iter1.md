# Question

When did the person who said "Cogito, ergo sum." die?

# Ground Truth Answer

February 11, 1650

# Model Prediction


The phrase "Cogito, ergo sum." is famous worldwide and is often translated into English as "I think, therefore I am." This statement is widely attributed to the French philosopher René Descartes, who played a pivotal role in the philosophical and scientific revolutions of the early modern period. The question pertains to the date of death of this influential thinker.

René Descartes was born on March 31, 1596, in La Haye en Touraine, France. Over his lifetime, he made significant contributions to mathematics, philosophy, and science, most notably through his development of Cartesian geometry and his foundational work in rationalism.

Descartes' final years were marked by his invitation to the court of Queen Christina of Sweden, where he served as her personal tutor in philosophy. At the time, Sweden was experiencing harsh winters, and Descartes' health began to decline rapidly due to the severe climate and possibly complications from pneumonia.

On February 11, 1650, René Descartes died in Stockholm, Sweden, at the age of 53. His passing occurred amidst the cold conditions of the Swedish winter, which had adversely affected his already fragile health. Although the calendar systems of different European nations varied at the time (Julian versus Gregorian), most historical accounts agree that February 11, 1650, is the accepted date of his death according to the calendars then in use in Sweden and France.

To summarize:

- **Philosopher:** René Descartes
- **Famous Statement:** "Cogito, ergo sum." ("I think, therefore I am.")
- **Date of Death:** February 11, 1650
- **Place of Death:** Stockholm, Sweden
- **Cause of Death:** Likely pneumonia exacerbated by the climate

Descartes' legacy endures in contemporary philosophy, mathematics, and scientific thought. His approach to systematic doubt and his principle of self-existence through thinking continue to influence debates about knowledge, consciousness, and the foundations of reason.
