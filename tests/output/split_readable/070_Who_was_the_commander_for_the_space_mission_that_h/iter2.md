# Question

Who was the commander for the space mission that had the first spacewalk?

# Ground Truth Answer

Pavel Belyayev

# Model Prediction


The first-ever spacewalk, also known as an extravehicular activity (EVA), was accomplished by Soviet cosmonaut Alexei Leonov during the Voskhod 2 mission. This milestone event took place on March 18, 1965. While Alexei Leonov made history by exiting the capsule and floating freely in space for about ten minutes, the role of commander for this pioneering mission was held by another skilled Soviet cosmonaut: Pavel Romanovich Belyayev.

## Background of the Mission

The Voskhod 2 mission was part of the Soviet Union’s efforts in the Space Race against the United States. Unlike its predecessor, the Voskhod 1 mission, Voskhod 2 carried two crew members, making it one of the early multi-person spacecraft. The main objective was to demonstrate that humans could survive and work outside their spacecraft, which was crucial for future space exploration, including possible lunar landings or constructing space stations.

## The Crew of Voskhod 2

- **Pavel Romanovich Belyayev:** Commander
- **Alexei Arkhipovich Leonov:** Pilot/First Spacewalker

Pavel Belyayev had extensive experience as a pilot and was selected for the mission due to his expertise and leadership skills. He was responsible for the overall management of the spacecraft, monitoring systems, guiding the mission profile, and ensuring both crewmembers' safety throughout the flight. His responsibilities included critical decisions during complex phases such as launch, orbital operations, and reentry.

On the other hand, Alexei Leonov was tasked with performing the historic spacewalk. His suit, life support systems, and exit from the capsule were all managed under Pavel Belyayev's command. The partnership between Belyayev and Leonov was crucial—Belyayev maintained control of the spacecraft at all times, especially during Leonov's dangerous EVA when the spacecraft’s systems and orientation needed constant attention.

## Significance of the First Spacewalk

Leonov’s EVA lasted approximately ten minutes and proved that humans could operate outside the protective confines of a spacecraft. However, the achievement was not without risks. During his exit, Leonov encountered difficulties due to his spacesuit expanding in the vacuum of space, making it challenging to re-enter the airlock. Back inside the capsule, Belyayev played a vital role by managing the spacecraft’s attitude and assisting Leonov during the critical re-docking process.

This accomplishment opened new possibilities for future space missions involving repairs, construction in orbit, and scientific experiments. It also intensified the competition between the United States and the Soviet Union, leading to rapid advancements in space technology.

## Legacy

The successful completion of the Voskhod 2 mission contributed significantly to the evolution of human spaceflight. It demonstrated that spacewalking was feasible, providing essential experience for subsequent missions, including those that would involve constructing the International Space Station and sending astronauts to the Moon.

Pavel Belyayev’s role as commander exemplified the importance of teamwork and effective leadership in high-risk explorations. His calmness and technical proficiency ensured that the mission objectives were met safely despite unexpected challenges.

In conclusion, while Alexei Leonov became famous for stepping outside the capsule into space, it was Pavel Belyayev who led and directed the Voskhod 2 mission as its commander. Their joint efforts remain a celebrated chapter in the history of space exploration.
