### Pac-Man

- **First Released:** 1980
- **Notable Features:** Arcade roots, global phenomenon, numerous ports and spin-offs
- **Recent Titles:** Pac-Man 99 (2021), Pac-Man Museum+ (2020), continued mobile games and anniversary editions
- **Producer:** Toru Iwatani (creator) at Namco (now Bandai Namco Entertainment)

### Space Invaders

- **First Released:** 1978
- **Notable Features:** Influential in establishing the shooter genre, major cultural impact
- **Recent Titles:** Space Invaders Infinity Gene (2009), NEAR (2021)
- **Producer:** Toshihiko Fujiwara / Taito

### Donkey Kong/Mario

- **First Released:** 1981 (Donkey Kong), 1985 (Super Mario Bros.)
- **Notable Features:** Birthplace of iconic characters Mario and Donkey Kong, enduring popularity
- **Recent Titles:** Mario + Rabbids Kingdom Battle (2023), Mario Kart Tour (2019)
- **Producer:** Shigeru Miyamoto at Nintendo

### Tetris

- **First Released:** 1984
- **Notable Features:** Simplicity, puzzle mastery, cross-platform presence
- **Recent Titles:** Tetris Effect (2020), Tetris 99 (2019)
- **Producer:** Alexey Pajitnov, licensed to various publishers

## Analysis and Comparison

**Pac-Man** stands out as the longest-running video game franchise when considering both the duration and frequency of new content. Although Space Invaders was released earlier (1978), its sequels and adaptations are less frequent compared to Pac-Man. Similarly, the Mario franchise is globally renowned but started a year after Pac-Man.

Pac-Man’s legacy encompasses hundreds of variations, mobile adaptations, competitive tournaments, and special anniversary events. Namco, as the producer, leveraged its IP to ensure the franchise stayed relevant through technological advancements—such as introducing Pac-Man 99, a battle royale mobile game.

Moreover, while Tetris is widely distributed and remains active, it lacks the same breadth of direct sequels and spin-offs. Similarly, Nintendo’s Mario franchise is newer but is incredibly successful, especially in terms of sales and recognition.

## Details About Pac-Man and Its Producer

### History of the Franchise

Pac-Man began as an arcade game in 1980, designed by Toru Iwatani at Namco. Its simple yet compelling gameplay quickly captured players worldwide. Over the years, Namco expanded the universe with:

- Ms. Pac-Man (1981)
- Pac-Man Plus (1982)
- Pac-Land (1984)
- Super Pac-Man (1982)
- Pac-Man World (1996, for PlayStation)
- Pac-Man Battle Royale (2010)
- Pac-Man Championship Edition DX (2010)
- Pac-Man 99 (2021, multiplayer online battle royale)
- Pac-Man Museum+ (2020)
- Numerous mobile versions and updates

Each generation embraced new platforms, including consoles, PC, mobile devices, and even VR.

### Role of Namco and Toru Iwatani

Toru Iwatani, as the creator and driving force behind Pac-Man, conceptualized the game while working for Namco. He aimed to craft a game accessible to all, regardless of skill level, resulting in Pac-Man’s enduring appeal.

Namco, later renamed Bandai Namco Entertainment, maintained oversight of the franchise, licensing, updating, and developing new iterations. The company’s commitment ensured the franchise’s survival through changing technologies and gaming trends.

## Legacy and Cultural Impact

Pac-Man is often cited as one of the most recognizable icons in gaming history. It transcended entertainment, appearing in films, television shows, merchandise, and even academic discourse regarding video game culture. Anniversaries and pop-culture homages—such as Google Doodles or retro gaming conventions—further reinforce its significance.

By continuously evolving, Pac-Man set benchmarks for long-term engagement in gaming, showing how careful stewardship of intellectual property can sustain popularity across generations.
