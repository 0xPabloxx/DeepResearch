The title of the longest-running video game franchise is a subject of debate among gaming historians, but most commonly the distinction goes to **Space Invaders**, produced by Taito Corporation. Developed by Tomohiro Nishikado, Space Invaders made its debut in 1978 and is widely regarded as one of the seminal milestones in the history of video games. Its legacy has been maintained through multiple sequels, spin-offs, remakes, and adaptations across various platforms over more than four decades, making it arguably the oldest and most enduring video game franchise.

## Historical Background

**Taito Corporation** created Space Invaders in 1978, marking a turning point in the arcade industry. Its innovative gameplay mechanics—where players defend against waves of descending aliens—catalyzed a surge in the popularity of video arcades worldwide. Within just a few years, Space Invaders became a cultural phenomenon, spawning numerous imitators and influencing countless game developers. 

Over the years, Taito continued the franchise with follow-up titles and experiments, including:
- **Space Invaders II** (1980): Introduced additional features and enemies.
- **Space Invaders Part II** (1982): Enhanced gameplay mechanics and visuals.
- **Space Invaders DX** (1994): A modernized reimagining for arcade machines.
- **Space Invaders Pocket** (1998): A handheld adaptation.
- **Space Invaders Invincible Collection** (2016): Compiles classic titles for modern consoles.

In recent times, the franchise continues to adapt to new trends, with releases such as *Space Invaders Infinity Gene* (2009), which featured updated graphics and mechanics, and various mobile and downloadable versions. These efforts keep Space Invaders relevant in contemporary gaming culture.

## The Role of the Producer

When discussing who "produced" the longest-running franchise, it's important to recognize both the corporate entity and the individual creators. In the case of Space Invaders, Tomohiro Nishikado was the principal developer and visionary behind the original game, with Taito Corporation serving as the publisher and ongoing steward of the franchise. After Taito was acquired by Square Enix, the rights and stewardship of the brand shifted, but the original legacy remains rooted in Taito’s innovations.

Other franchises similarly involve a mix of creative individuals and companies. For instance, Mario is associated with Shigeru Miyamoto at Nintendo, and Pac-Man with Toru Iwatani at Namco. However, in terms of pure longevity, Space Invaders stands unchallenged due to its origin date and sustained activity.

## Enduring Legacy

The persistence of Space Invaders as a franchise highlights several key factors in the survival and success of video game series:
- **Innovation:** The core gameplay introduced new mechanics and concepts still used today.
- **Adaptability:** Continuous updates and cross-platform releases have kept the franchise appealing to changing audiences.
- **Cultural Impact:** Space Invaders’ influence helped shape the broader landscape of popular culture around video games.

Despite the emergence of new franchises every year, none have maintained consistent new entries since before 1978. Space Invaders remains a benchmark for both video game history and business longevity.

## Conclusion

In summary, the longest-running video game franchise is **Space Invaders**, produced by **Taito Corporation** (with Tomohiro Nishikado as its creator). With its groundbreaking release in 1978 and continued expansion into new markets and technologies, Space Invaders sets the standard for endurance in the gaming industry. While other franchises like Pac-Man, Mario, and Tetris also enjoy long histories, none match the pioneering status and duration of Space Invaders.
