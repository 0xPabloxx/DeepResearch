## Who Was the Father of Information Theory?

Claude Shannon is widely regarded as the father of information theory due to his pioneering work during the mid-twentieth century. His mathematical framework quantified the concepts of information, introduced the idea of the bit as a fundamental unit, and provided essential tools for the digital revolution—including error correction, data compression, and channel capacity theory.

Before Shannon, other scientists and engineers such as Harry Nyquist and Ralph Hartley had explored aspects of communication and signal processing, but none synthesized the field in the way Shannon did. His work bridged disciplines including electrical engineering, mathematics, statistics, and computer science, providing a foundational language and mathematics still in use today.
