## Who Was the Father of Information Theory?

Claude Shannon is hailed as the founder of information theory. Born in 1916 in Petoskey, Michigan, Shannon revolutionized electrical engineering and computer science with his 1948 paper “A Mathematical Theory of Communication.” He introduced concepts such as the bit as a unit of information, quantifying entropy in communication, and showing how any message, regardless of form—be it speech, music, text, or image—could be converted into binary code and transmitted efficiently and reliably across noisy channels.

Shannon’s contributions underpin the digital age, enabling everything from error-correcting codes essential for internet security to the algorithms powering wireless communication and data compression.
