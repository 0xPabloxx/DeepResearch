## The Namesake: Gerald R. Ford

Gerald R. Ford was born in 1913 and served as a U.S. Congressman, Vice President, and then President following the resignation of Richard Nixon. Before his presidency, he had a distinguished military record during World War II, serving in the U.S. Navy. His career spanned significant periods in American history, including the Cold War, and he was known for his commitment to public service and national unity.

Ford’s reputation as a steady and principled leader made him a fitting namesake for America’s newest aircraft carrier. Naming this major naval asset after a former president underscores the importance of leadership, service, and legacy in the U.S. Navy.

## Historical Context of Carrier Naming

Traditionally, U.S. aircraft carriers have been named after prominent figures in American history, including presidents, admirals, and other leaders whose contributions are deemed significant to the nation. For example, the Nimitz-class carriers are named after Admiral Chester W. Nimitz, a key commander during World War II.

The choice to name the USS Gerald R. Ford reflects the tradition of honoring those who have held high office or demonstrated outstanding leadership. In this case, Gerald R. Ford’s service and post-presidential contributions were recognized by the Navy through this honor.
