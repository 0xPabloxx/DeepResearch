# Question

When did the war that Neil Armstrong served in end?

# Ground Truth Answer

July 27, 1953

# Model Prediction


Neil Armstrong, widely recognized as the first person to walk on the moon during the Apollo 11 mission in 1969, had a distinguished career prior to his role at NASA. Before becoming an astronaut, Armstrong served as a naval aviator in the United States Navy during the Korean War.

Armstrong enlisted in the Navy in 1949 and completed his flight training in 1950. Shortly after becoming a naval aviator, he was deployed to active combat service during the Korean War. Specifically, Armstrong flew 78 combat missions over Korea while serving aboard the USS Enterprise. His time in combat significantly shaped his experience as a pilot and contributed to his later success as an astronaut.

The Korean War began on June 25, 1950, following North Korean forces crossing the 38th parallel and invading South Korea. The conflict quickly drew in international forces, with the United Nations commanding a coalition that included the United States, under President Harry S. Truman’s leadership.

As tensions escalated along the peninsula, Neil Armstrong was called to serve and became one of many young men who fought during this crucial period in the early Cold War era. His responsibilities included flying F9F Panther jets, which were frequently used for ground-attack and escort missions against enemy targets. Armstrong’s military service provided him with critical skills in aircraft handling, crisis management, and teamwork—all invaluable traits that would later define his work in space exploration.

Despite fierce resistance and numerous battles, the fighting between North and South Korea reached a stalemate by mid-1953. An armistice agreement, negotiated between the representatives of the United Nations Command and the forces of North Korea and China, finally brought hostilities to a halt. The Korean War officially ended when the Armistice Agreement was signed on July 27, 1953. This agreement did not constitute a formal peace treaty, so technically the two Koreas remain in a state of war today. However, for practical purposes and historical reference, July 27, 1953, marks the end of active combat operations in the conflict.

In summary, the war that Neil Armstrong served in—namely, the Korean War—ended on July 27, 1953. His contributions as a naval aviator during this conflict are a testament to his courage and skill, qualities that would later propel him onto the world stage as an iconic figure in the history of space exploration.
