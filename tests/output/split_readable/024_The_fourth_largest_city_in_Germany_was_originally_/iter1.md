The fourth largest city in Germany is Cologne (German: Köln). Originally, it had a very different name than it does today. When Cologne was founded by the Romans in 38 AD, it was known as "Colonia Claudia Ara Agrippinensium." This lengthy Latin name reflected both the political importance of the settlement and the high status of its founder.

**Historical Background**

Cologne began as a Roman military outpost but rapidly grew into one of the most significant urban centers in the Roman Empire north of the Alps. The city's full name was given in honor of the Roman Emperor Claudius, who provided the authority for the establishment of the colony, and his wife, Agrippina the Younger, who was born in the region. The term "Ara Agrippinensium" refers to an altar built in honor of Agrippina.

The abbreviated form, "Colonia," comes from "Colonia Claudia," meaning "Colony of Claudius." Over time, this abbreviated name became the basis for Cologne’s current German name, “Köln.”

**Origins of the Name**

Before it became "Colonia Claudia Ara Agrippinensium," the area that is now Cologne was inhabited by Celtic tribes, specifically the Ubii people. Their settlement was known as Oppidum Ubiorum. After the Roman conquest, the Romans established a new city nearby, which replaced the earlier settlement.

The founding of the city as a Roman colony brought wealth, culture, and strategic importance to the area. As the years passed, the city flourished, becoming a major religious, commercial, and administrative center in the Roman Empire and later in medieval Europe.

**Significance of the Name**

The title "Colonia Claudia Ara Agrippinensium" (CCA or Colonia Agrippinensium) underscores several key historical elements:

- **Imperial sponsorship:** The naming honors the Roman emperor and empress, reflecting the city's official status as a Roman colony.
- **Religious aspect:** The "Ara" (altar) symbolized devotion to the imperial cult, which was integral to Roman governance and society.
- **Local heritage:** The inclusion of "Agrippinensium" acknowledges the contributions and significance of Agrippina, who played a notable role in the history of the region.

Over centuries, the city's name evolved, gradually shifting from the formal Latin name to simpler references like "Colonia," which then transformed into the modern German "Köln."
