The country most widely recognized as the birthplace of yoga is India. Evidence suggests that yoga began during the Indus Valley Civilization, approximately 5,000 years ago. Over centuries, it developed into various forms, influenced by Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism, before becoming widely known and practiced across the globe. Ancient texts such as the Vedas, Upanishads, Bhagavad Gita, and Patanjali’s Yoga Sutras are foundational to the development and understanding of yoga.

India’s rich cultural heritage and spiritual tradition make it not only the originator of yoga but also its principal guardian and interpreter for millennia. The land is home to countless temples, ashrams, and meditation centers, all dedicated to the study and teaching of yoga. Renowned yogis, gurus, and saints who have shaped yoga’s evolution lived and taught primarily in India.

The capital city of India is New Delhi. Officially, New Delhi serves as the national capital, located in the northern part of the country and forming the heart of the Indian Union Territory of Delhi. It is a bustling metropolis, functioning as the center of government, commerce, education, and culture in India. New Delhi houses the Parliament House, major administrative offices, embassies, and iconic monuments such as India Gate and Rashtrapati Bhavan.

- The capital city of India is New Delhi.
