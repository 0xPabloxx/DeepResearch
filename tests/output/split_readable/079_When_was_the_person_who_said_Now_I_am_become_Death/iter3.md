J. Robert Oppenheimer was born on July 22, 1904, in New York City. He became one of the leading theoretical physicists of the 20th century and was instrumental in the development of atomic weapons during World War II. His leadership and intellect made him a pivotal figure in scientific history, earning him titles such as “father of the atomic bomb.” However, the profound moral weight of his accomplishment led him to reflect on the transformative and destructive power humanity had unleashed, encapsulated in his quotation from the Bhagavad Gita.

After the war, Oppenheimer became an advocate for international control over nuclear energy and expressed deep concerns about the future ramifications of nuclear arms. His legacy is complex, marked by both monumental scientific achievement and a lifelong contemplation of the ethical dilemmas faced by scientists whose discoveries have global consequences.
