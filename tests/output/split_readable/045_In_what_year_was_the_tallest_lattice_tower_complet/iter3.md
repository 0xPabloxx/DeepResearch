The tallest lattice tower in the world is Tokyo Tower, located in Tokyo, Japan. This iconic structure is composed of a lattice of steel, giving it a distinctive and recognizable appearance. ### Historical Context

Tokyo Tower was designed by architect Tachu Naito and is inspired by the Eiffel Tower in Paris. While the Eiffel Tower, completed in 1889, set the standard for lattice towers and remains a symbol of architectural ingenuity, Tokyo Tower surpassed it in height by 3 meters. This achievement cemented Tokyo Tower’s status as the world’s tallest lattice tower at the time of its completion.

After Tokyo Tower was finished, several other towers reached or exceeded its height, but most were constructed with materials other than lattice steel—such as concrete or composite materials—and were designed for different purposes, such as broadcasting, tourism, or a blend of functions. For example, the CN Tower in Canada and the Ostankino Tower in Moscow, though impressive, are primarily concrete structures rather than true lattice towers. Even though modern marvels such as the Burj Khalifa and the Shanghai Tower are much taller, they are skyscrapers and do not fit the traditional definition of a lattice tower.

### What is a Lattice Tower?

A lattice tower is a freestanding structure made predominantly of interconnected steel beams arranged in a crisscross pattern, often forming triangles or other stable geometric shapes. This design provides both strength and flexibility, allowing the tower to withstand environmental forces such as wind and seismic activity. Historically, lattice towers have been used for broadcasting and observation, with the Eiffel Tower serving as the archetype. The lattice design allows for significant height while keeping the structure relatively lightweight compared to solid forms.
