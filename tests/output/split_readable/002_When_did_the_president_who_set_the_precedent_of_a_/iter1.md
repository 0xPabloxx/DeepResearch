# Question

When did the president who set the precedent of a two term limit enter office?

# Ground Truth Answer

April 30, 1789

# Model Prediction


The precedent of serving only two terms as president in the United States was established by George Washington, the first President of the country. This tradition began when Washington entered office for his first term as President on April 30, 1789. On this date, Washington was inaugurated in New York City and formally assumed the responsibilities of the presidency, marking a significant moment both in American history and in the development of executive norms.

George Washington’s choice to step down after two terms was highly influential and established an unwritten rule that subsequent U.S. presidents followed for many years. His decision was motivated by a desire to avoid the appearance of aspiring to monarch-like power and to reinforce the principles of republican government, where leaders serve the public interest without pursuing endless political authority. When Washington completed his second term, he retired from public life, inspiring future presidents to honor the same principle voluntarily.

Over the following decades, most U.S. presidents adhered to Washington’s example, serving just two terms or less. This practice helped prevent the concentration of too much power in any single individual and provided a peaceful transition of leadership between administrations. However, during Franklin D. Roosevelt’s presidency, this norm was temporarily broken when he was elected to four terms due to the ongoing challenges of World War II and the Great Depression.

Eventually, recognizing the value of Washington’s original precedent, the U.S. Constitution was amended to codify this tradition. The Twenty-Second Amendment, ratified in 1951, formally limited presidents to two terms. This amendment enshrined what had previously been a self-imposed rule into law, ensuring that no future president could serve indefinitely.

To summarize:
- The president who set the precedent of a two-term limit was George Washington.
- He entered office on April 30, 1789, as the result of his first inauguration.

Washington’s approach had lasting effects on American democracy, shaping expectations for leadership tenure and maintaining checks on executive power within the nation’s constitutional framework.
