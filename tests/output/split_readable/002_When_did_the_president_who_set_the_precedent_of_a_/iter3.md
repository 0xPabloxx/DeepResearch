# Question

When did the president who set the precedent of a two term limit enter office?

# Ground Truth Answer

April 30, 1789

# Model Prediction


The president who set the precedent of a two-term limit in the United States was George Washington. After serving as the first President of the country, he established the tradition of stepping down after two terms, setting an example that would be followed by his successors for over a century. This tradition became such a foundational aspect of American political culture that it was later codified into law with the passage of the 22nd Amendment to the United States Constitution, which formally limited presidents to two elected terms in office.

## George Washington’s Entry into Office

George Washington officially entered office as the first President of the United States on April 30, 1789. On this date, he took the presidential oath of office at Federal Hall in New York City, marking the formal beginning of his administration. His assumption of the presidency was a historic event, not only because he was the first to hold the new office under the U.S. Constitution but also because the actions he undertook during his tenure would influence the trajectory of the nation's leadership practices.

## The Significance of Washington’s Two-Term Precedent

Washington’s decision to serve only two terms was highly deliberate. Though the Constitution did not explicitly limit the number of terms a president could serve, Washington chose not to seek a third term, viewing a lengthy tenure in power as potentially dangerous to democratic principles. He believed strongly in the idea of rotational leadership, to prevent the concentration of power and to allow fresh perspectives to shape the government.

His voluntary retirement after two terms established an unwritten rule or tradition within American politics. This precedent was respected by all subsequent presidents until Franklin D. Roosevelt’s election to an unprecedented fourth term during the Great Depression and World War II. After Roosevelt’s death, the need to formally codify this tradition led to the ratification of the 22nd Amendment in 1951, which enshrined the two-term limit as part of the constitutional framework.

## Lasting Impact of Washington’s Decision

By establishing the two-term precedent, Washington played a pivotal role in shaping the norms of the American presidency. His choice reflected concerns about autocracy and the importance of peaceful transfer of power. For more than 150 years, this tradition helped ensure political stability and continuity, preventing any single individual from amassing excessive control over the executive branch.

After decades of adherence to this norm, the departure from it by Franklin D. Roosevelt prompted lawmakers to act to prevent its recurrence. The eventual adoption of the 22nd Amendment underscored the lasting influence of Washington’s original decision.

To directly answer your question: George Washington, who set the precedent of a two-term limit for U.S. presidents, entered office on April 30, 1789. His voluntary departure after two terms became the cornerstone of presidential succession practices and had profound implications for American governance, ultimately leading to a constitutional amendment that institutionalized this principle.
