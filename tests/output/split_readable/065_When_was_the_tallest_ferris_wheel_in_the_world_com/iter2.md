## Construction Timeline and Completion

Construction on Ain Dubai began in December 2015, following the original announcement of the project. The project quickly gained attention due to its ambitious scale and the vision behind transforming Bluewaters Island into a major tourist destination. The primary phases of construction were:

| Milestone                          | Date                    |
|-------------------------------------|-------------------------|

After this, the focus shifted to installing passenger capsules, integrating advanced technology, ensuring safety standards, and completing interior fit-outs.

Despite reaching structural completion in 2020, the process extended because of numerous technical challenges, global events such as the pandemic, and high design standards. Ultimately, Ain Dubai officially opened to the public on December 30, 2023.

## Overview of Observation Wheels Held World Record

Prior to the official opening of Ain Dubai, several other observation wheels held the record for tallest Ferris wheel. Below is a chronological overview:

| Wheel Name            | Location           | Height (meters) | Year Completed |
|----------------------|--------------------|-----------------|---------------|
| London Eye           | London, UK         | 135             | 2000          |
| Singapore Flyer      | Singapore          | 165             | 2008          |
| High Roller          | Las Vegas, USA     | 167.6           | 2014          |
| Ain Dubai            | Dubai, UAE         | 250             | 2023          |

This progression highlights the technological advancements and ambition in creating larger and more sophisticated observation wheels over the past two decades.

## Significance of Ain Dubai

Ain Dubai is not just notable for its size; it also features cutting-edge engineering and offers passengers an unparalleled experience. Each capsule is climate-controlled, capable of holding up to 40 people, and provides panoramic views of Dubai and beyond. The wheel’s speed is carefully controlled, allowing for smooth boarding and disembarkation while maximizing ride time at the top for sightseeing.

The construction of Ain Dubai represents a milestone in the history of amusement rides. It showcases the progress in materials science, aerodynamics, and digital monitoring systems required to safely support such a massive rotating structure. Additionally, it has become a symbol of Dubai’s ongoing efforts to attract international tourists and reinforce its status as a global city.

## Comparison to Previous Record Holders

Before Ain Dubai, the High Roller in Las Vegas held the record, standing at 167.6 meters and opening in March 2014. The High Roller had the distinction of being the tallest at the time for almost ten years, demonstrating the shift of ambitions in observation wheel design toward increasing scale and passenger capacity.

While the Singapore Flyer (165 meters, opened in 2008) and the London Eye (135 meters, opened in 2000) previously set records, each new wheel raised the bar for innovation and spectacle. Ain Dubai now surpasses all previous models not only in height but also in overall guest experience, engineering complexity, and visual impact.

## Conclusion

In summary, the world’s tallest Ferris wheel—the Ain Dubai—was structurally completed in 2020, but officially opened to the public on December 30, 2023. Its creation marks a new chapter in the history of observation wheels and sets a new standard for future projects globally. As of today, it remains the largest and most technologically advanced Ferris wheel ever built, offering visitors an unmatched view of one of the world’s most iconic cities.
