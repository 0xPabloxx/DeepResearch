## Historical Context and Early Ferris Wheels

The concept of the giant Ferris wheel began in the late 19th century during the era of grand expositions and technological marvels. The first truly monumental Ferris wheel was constructed for the 1893 World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago. Designed by George Washington Gale Ferris Jr., this original Ferris wheel stood as an engineering feat and symbolized American innovation. It had a diameter of 250 feet (approximately 76 meters) and rose 264 feet above the ground, becoming the tallest amusement ride of its day. Its construction was completed in 1893, marking the beginning of a tradition of building ever-taller observation wheels.

## Modern Ferris Wheels and Global Competition

As cities around the world sought iconic attractions, massive Ferris wheels became symbols of modernity and progress. Several cities invested in these structures, each aiming to claim the title of the world’s tallest observation wheel. Here is a timeline detailing major milestones:

### London Eye

The London Eye, originally called the Millennium Wheel, was designed to celebrate the millennium and has been a defining part of London's skyline since its completion in 1999, with public operations starting in 2000. Standing at 135 meters (443 feet), it held the record until larger wheels were constructed.

### Star of Nanchang and Singapore Flyer

With the rapid development in Asia, new contenders emerged. The Star of Nanchang, completed in 2006 in China, claimed the tallest title at 160 meters. Soon after, the Singapore Flyer surpassed this with a height of 165 meters, opening to the public in 2008.

### High Roller in Las Vegas

The latest and current record holder is the High Roller in Las Vegas, United States. Completed in 2014, it stands at 167.6 meters (550 feet), making it the tallest Ferris wheel in the world as of 2025. The High Roller's construction finished in early 2014, and it officially opened to the public on March 31, 2014. Its enormous size allows visitors to enjoy panoramic views over the Las Vegas Strip and the surrounding desert landscape. This achievement represents the culmination of ongoing efforts by engineers, architects, and urban planners to push the boundaries of what is possible in large-scale amusements.

## Other Notable Projects

Various cities have proposed or started construction on even larger Ferris wheels but many remain unbuilt. Examples include plans for the New York Wheel in the United States and the Dubai Eye in the United Arab Emirates, but neither has reached completion. As of now, the High Roller remains the largest operational Ferris wheel.
