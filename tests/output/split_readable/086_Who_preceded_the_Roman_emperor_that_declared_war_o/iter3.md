His death in AD 37 created a sudden transition to the throne for his grand-nephew and adopted son, Caligula.

Tiberius did not engage in any well-known acts that could be described as a “declaration of war on the sea.” Instead, his policies focused on maintaining the stability and boundaries of the empire through diplomacy and conventional warfare.
