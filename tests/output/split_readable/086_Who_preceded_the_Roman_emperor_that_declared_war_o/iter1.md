## The Emperor Who Declared War on the Sea

Among the various Roman emperors, Caligula—whose full name was Gaius Caesar Augustus Germanicus—is most famously associated with the concept of declaring war on the sea. ## Context and Significance

Caligula’s rule, lasting from AD 37 to AD 41, is characterized by increasing mental instability and erratic behavior. His declaration of war on the sea is one of the most memorable anecdotes illustrating his madness, as it represented a futile and comical attempt to achieve personal satisfaction through farcical means rather than actual conquest or public service.
