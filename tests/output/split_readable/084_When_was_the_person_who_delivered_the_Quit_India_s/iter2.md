# Question

# Ground Truth Answer

# Model Prediction


The person who delivered the "Quit India" speech is Mahatma Gandhi, one of the most influential leaders in Indian history. On August 8, 1942, at the All India Congress Committee session in Bombay, Gandhi delivered the famous "Quit India" speech, calling for immediate independence from British colonial rule. This speech marked the beginning of the Quit India Movement, a mass civil disobedience movement that played a pivotal role in India's struggle for freedom.

Mahatma Gandhi, whose full name was Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi, was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, a coastal town in present-day Gujarat, India. He was born into a Hindu Modh family and went on to become a central figure in India's independence movement through his philosophy of nonviolent resistance, known as Satyagraha.

### Details about Mahatma Gandhi’s Birth

- **Full Name:** Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi
- **Date of Birth:** October 2, 1869
- **Place of Birth:** Porbandar, Gujarat, India (then part of British India)

Gandhi’s birthplace, Porbandar, is located in western India along the Arabian Sea. The region was then a princely state under British administration. His early life was shaped by the cultural and social traditions of Gujarat, which influenced his later philosophies and principles.

### Significance of the “Quit India” Speech

The “Quit India” speech delivered by Gandhi on August 8, 1942, was a defining moment in Indian history. In his impassioned address, Gandhi urged Indians to adopt a campaign of nonviolent resistance against British colonial rule, famously concluding with the phrase “Do or Die.” This speech mobilized millions of people across India and led to widespread protests, arrests, and acts of civil disobedience.

While other prominent leaders were involved in the broader independence movement—such as Jawaharlal Nehru and Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel—Gandhi was the principal leader and the primary architect of the nonviolent strategy. He remains the most widely recognized figure associated with the “Quit India” Movement.

### Legacy of Mahatma Gandhi

Gandhi’s leadership, commitment to truth and peace, and ability to inspire ordinary people to participate in the fight for freedom made him a global symbol of resistance and reconciliation. His birthday, October 2, is celebrated annually in India as Gandhi Jayanti, a national holiday, and on the international stage as the International Day of Non-Violence.
