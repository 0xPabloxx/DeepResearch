# Model Prediction


The person who delivered the famous "Quit India" speech was Mahatma Gandhi, one of the most significant leaders in Indian history. This speech was delivered on August 8, 1942, during a critical phase of India’s struggle for independence from British colonial rule. The "Quit India" speech became a turning point in the movement, galvanizing millions to demand an end to British dominion over India.

Mahatma Gandhi was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, which is located in present-day Gujarat, India. His birthdate is widely celebrated as Gandhi Jayanti, a national holiday in India, honoring his contributions to the nation and his philosophy of nonviolent resistance.

Gandhi's life and leadership played a crucial role in shaping India’s independence movement. His approach of satyagraha (nonviolent resistance) inspired countless people, both in India and worldwide, to challenge injustice through peaceful means. During the "Quit India" movement, Gandhi called for “Do or Die” in pursuit of independence, urging all Indians to come together in unity and determination to achieve freedom.

The context of the "Quit India" speech was especially important because it came at a time when World War II was underway and global attention was focused elsewhere. Despite these challenges, Gandhi mobilized the masses, leading to widespread civil disobedience across India. The speech and subsequent movement marked a decisive shift from earlier, more gradualist approaches to demanding immediate and unconditional independence.

To summarize:

- **Person who delivered the "Quit India" speech:** Mahatma Gandhi
- **Date of birth:** October 2, 1869
- **Place of birth:** Porbandar, Gujarat, British India

Gandhi’s legacy continues to be influential globally, and his birthday is commemorated as a day to reflect on peace, justice, and the power of nonviolent protest. The "Quit India" speech remains a symbol of courage and conviction in the face of adversity.
