Historically, it was introduced by the Internet Assigned Numbers Authority (IANA) for United States military use, and many other countries have followed suit using .mil for their military organizations as well.

The .mil TLD has been widely recognized as the de facto designation for military-related institutions across several countries, most notably the United States. This makes it unique among all top level domains, as it’s restricted to official military bodies and requires approval for use.
