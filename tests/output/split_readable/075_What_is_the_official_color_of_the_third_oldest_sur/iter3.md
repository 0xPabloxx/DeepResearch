The official color of the University of Cambridge is **Cambridge Blue**. This unique pale cyan shade is instantly recognizable and serves as a powerful symbol of the university’s heritage and sporting identity, particularly in events like the Boat Race against Oxford University. Over time, Cambridge Blue has become synonymous with intellectual pursuit, tradition, and prestige.

### Description and Usage of Cambridge Blue

Cambridge Blue is visually lighter than Oxford Blue, the deep navy favored by the University of Oxford. Cambridge Blue occupies a space between sky blue and light cyan, giving it a distinctive yet subtle character.

While exact specifications may vary slightly between departments and branding materials, Cambridge Blue is typically described as a pastel cyan or light aqua. The color has appeared in official publications, logos, and merchandise, and is even used in the university’s flag and ceremonial items.

### Cultural Significance of Cambridge Blue

Cambridge Blue is closely tied to the university’s history and rivalry with Oxford. The color was adopted in the late 19th century as part of an effort to create a strong visual identity, particularly for rowing. Its adoption persists today as a symbol of academic excellence and tradition.

The color is most prominently displayed during the annual Boat Race, where teams wear garments and paddles in Cambridge Blue. It also features in university insignia, academic regalia, posters, campus signage, and digital content.

## Historical Context and Branding Evolution

The establishment of Cambridge Blue is rooted in both rivalry and institutional pride. Just as Oxford embraced a darker blue (Oxford Blue), Cambridge sought a shade that would distinguish itself—a choice inspired partly by the natural environment of East Anglia and partly by a desire to maintain a vibrant yet classical image.

Over time, Cambridge Blue has evolved into an essential element of the university’s global brand, representing innovation, history, and community.

## Why This Distinction Matters

Distinguishing “official color” adds depth to how universities present themselves globally. While some institutions focus solely on academics, others use their colors as integral parts of identity, especially when engaging with students, alumni, and the wider public. 

For Cambridge, Cambridge Blue is much more than a color—it’s a part of daily life, rallying cry, and source of pride for generations.

## Conclusion

In summary, the official color of the third oldest surviving university, the University of Cambridge, is **Cambridge Blue**.
