The phenomenon of superconductivity was discovered on April 8, 1911, by the Dutch physicist Heike Kamerlingh Onnes and his colleagues at Leiden University. This remarkable observation marked the first experimental evidence of superconductivity—a state in which certain materials conduct electricity without any energy loss.
