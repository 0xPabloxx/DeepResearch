**Background: The Founding of Robert Stephenson and Company**

Robert Stephenson and Company was established in 1823 in Newcastle upon Tyne, England. The company was founded by several prominent engineers and entrepreneurs, most notably Robert Stephenson and his father, George Stephenson, both of whom played pivotal roles in the development of early railway technology. Their goal was to manufacture innovative and reliable steam locomotives that could transform transportation and industry.

The founders' expertise and experience were instrumental in overcoming the technical challenges of producing engines that could reliably operate on rails and haul both freight and passengers over public railways. The timing of the company's foundation coincided with a period of intense technological change in Britain, as the Industrial Revolution created a demand for efficient means of moving goods and people across increasing distances.

**Significance of the First Passenger Steam Locomotive**

Before Robert Stephenson and Company, various steam-powered vehicles had been demonstrated, such as Richard Trevithick’s locomotive in Wales in 1804. However, these initial attempts were mostly confined to industrial settings or private lines and did not include regular passenger operations. The opening of the Stockton and Darlington Railway represented a watershed moment, as it was the world's first public railway to utilize steam locomotives for scheduled passenger and freight services.

Locomotion No. 1, constructed by Robert Stephenson and Company, proved capable of hauling heavy trains carrying both cargo and people—a feat that would fundamentally reshape economic and social life in the United Kingdom and eventually worldwide. The railway quickly became a model for subsequent projects, inspiring further investments in rail infrastructure.

**Robert Stephenson and Company’s Contribution to Railway History**

Upon its founding in 1823, Robert Stephenson and Company rapidly gained a reputation for engineering excellence and innovation. The firm continued to design and build locomotives for many major British and international railway lines. Their work included advancing steam technology, improving safety and efficiency, and influencing railway standards.

Their success at Stockton and Darlington was only the beginning. Later achievements included the construction of “Rocket,” another famous locomotive designed for the Rainhill Trials in 1829, which further cemented the company’s standing in railway engineering.

**Conclusion**

To directly answer the original question:  
Robert Stephenson and Company—the company responsible for constructing the first steam locomotive to carry passengers on a public rail line—was founded in 1823.

This founding date marks the establishment of a key player in the history of railways and transport, whose innovations laid the groundwork for the modern railway network.
