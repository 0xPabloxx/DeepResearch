## Background: The Discovery of Plutonium

Plutonium, an element with the atomic number 94 and symbol Pu, was first synthesized and discovered in 1940–1941 by a team led by Glenn T. Seaborg, Edwin McMillan, Joseph W. Kennedy, and Arthur Wahl. The research took place at the University of California, Berkeley. These scientists were studying nuclear reactions and transuranic elements using the cyclotron, a powerful particle accelerator, at Berkeley. Their work led to the identification of several new elements, including neptunium and plutonium.

## University of California, Berkeley: Founding and History

### Official Founding Date

The University of California system itself was established through an act of the California State Legislature. The specific date for the founding of UC Berkeley—the original campus and the institution's intellectual heart—is March 23, 1868. This is considered the official founding date when the university was chartered. However, the opening of the university’s first classes occurred later that year, on September 23, 1868.

This establishment marked the beginning of what would grow into one of the world's leading public research universities. Berkeley’s role in groundbreaking scientific research continued into the mid-20th century, notably with discoveries in nuclear chemistry and physics.

## Importance of UC Berkeley in Scientific Discovery

The university’s contributions extend far beyond the creation of a single element. The methods developed by Berkeley scientists during the search for new elements laid the foundation for much of modern nuclear science and technology. Plutonium’s discovery not only added a new member to the periodic table but also had significant implications for energy and military applications during and after World War II.
