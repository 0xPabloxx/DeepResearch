Lady Godiva was an Anglo-Saxon noblewoman famous for her legendary naked ride through Coventry, undertaken in protest against high taxes imposed by her husband. Her historical significance is closely tied to the political landscape of early medieval England. 

Her husband was Leofric, a prominent figure during the late Anglo-Saxon period. Leofric held the title of Earl of Mercia, one of the most powerful regions and kingdoms in England at the time. The Kingdom of Mercia was a major Anglo-Saxon kingdom, flourishing between the 6th and 9th centuries. By the time of Lady Godiva and her husband, Mercia remained a crucial center of political and military power in England, especially in the century leading up to the Norman Conquest.

Leofric was not only influential as the Earl of Mercia but also played a significant role in supporting religious institutions and charitable works. Together with Lady Godiva, he contributed generously to monasteries and churches throughout the region, including the rebuilding of the Abbey of St. Mary in Coventry.

The title “Earl” itself denotes a high-ranking noble in Anglo-Saxon society, second only to the king. Earls governed large territories, administered justice, raised armies, and managed resources. The role of Earl of Mercia was particularly prestigious because Mercia was once one of the dominant kingdoms in England, often vying for supremacy over other Anglo-Saxon territories.

To summarize:

- Lady Godiva’s husband was Leofric.
- Leofric was the Earl of Mercia.
- Mercia was one of the major Anglo-Saxon kingdoms in England, located in the central part of modern-day England.
