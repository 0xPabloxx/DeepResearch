**Background and Context**

NASA, established in 1958, is responsible for the United States' civilian space program and aeronautics research. The position of Administrator is the highest leadership role within NASA, overseeing its operations, mission planning, and public communications. As such, the Administrator plays a crucial part in directing major space endeavors like the Apollo missions.

Thomas O. Paine was appointed as NASA Administrator on October 22, 1968, following the resignation of his predecessor, James E. Webb. His tenure lasted until January 20, 1970. Notably, this period included the launch and successful completion of Apollo 11 in July 1969, which achieved humanity’s first crewed lunar landing with astronauts Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, and Michael Collins.

**Role and Contributions During Apollo 11**

As Administrator, Thomas O. Paine was responsible for guiding NASA through the final stages of President John F. Kennedy’s pledge to land humans on the Moon and return them safely to Earth before the end of the 1960s. Under his leadership, NASA managed significant technical challenges, system integrations, and operational risks associated with sending men to the Moon.

Some of Paine's notable actions during his administration include:
- Strengthening international partnerships and fostering cooperation with other nations’ space agencies.
- Advocating for continued funding and support for ambitious post-Apollo projects.
- Maintaining team morale and focus during high-pressure periods leading up to Apollo 11.

**Why Not James E. Webb?**

James E. Webb had been a transformative figure at NASA, significantly expanding its capabilities during the 1960s. However, he resigned in October 1968, just before the launch of Apollo 11. Thus, while Webb’s legacy set the stage for the success of Apollo 11, it was Thomas O. Paine who held the position of head of NASA during the actual execution of the mission.

This table clarifies that Paine was indeed in charge for Apollo 11, whereas Webb left a few months prior.

**Conclusion**

In summary, the head of NASA during the Apollo 11 mission was Thomas O. Paine.
