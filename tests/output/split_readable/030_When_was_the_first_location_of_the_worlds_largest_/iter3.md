# Model Prediction


The world’s largest coffeehouse chain is generally considered to be Starbucks. The opening of its very first location marks an important moment in the global coffee culture and commercial history.

Starbucks began its journey in Seattle, Washington. The original store was established on March 30, 1971, located at Pike Place Market. This initial cafe was set up by three partners: Jerry Baldwin, Zev Siegl, and Gordon Bowker. Their goal was not only to sell high-quality coffee beans, but also to introduce specialty coffees to Americans who were less familiar with premium roasted beans than Europeans.

In the early days, Starbucks did not serve brewed coffee; instead, it focused on selling whole bean coffee and tea. Over time, as coffee culture evolved and consumer preferences shifted towards ready-to-drink beverages, Starbucks expanded its menu to include espresso-based drinks and opened more locations worldwide.

The success of this first store laid the foundation for what would become an international phenomenon. Today, Starbucks operates thousands of outlets across over 80 countries, but the story always starts with that modest beginning in Seattle. The store at Pike Place Market remains symbolic, celebrated both by fans of the brand and those interested in business and cultural history.

In summary, the first location of the world’s largest coffeehouse chain—Starbucks—was opened on March 30, 1971, in Seattle’s Pike Place Market. This founding date is central to Starbucks’ history and reflects the start of its transformation from a local business into a leading global coffee brand.
