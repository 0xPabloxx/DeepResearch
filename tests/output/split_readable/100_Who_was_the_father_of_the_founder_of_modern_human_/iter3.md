### Vesalius' Contribution to Anatomy

The major reason Andreas Vesalius is celebrated as the founder of modern human anatomy lies in his insistence on direct observation through human dissection—a practice that was still risky and uncommon during his time. Vesalius advocated for empirical research, personally dissecting bodies and accurately documenting findings.

His landmark publication, "De humani corporis fabrica," is renowned for its comprehensive illustrations and detailed descriptions of the human body. The work overturned established doctrines and changed how anatomy was taught and practiced across Europe.
