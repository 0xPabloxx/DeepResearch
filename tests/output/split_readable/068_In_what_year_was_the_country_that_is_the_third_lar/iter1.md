**Background on Coffee Exports**

Coffee production in Colombia dates back to the early 19th century when small farmers began cultivating the crop across various regions, especially in mountainous areas with favorable climates. The industry supports millions of jobs directly and indirectly, including smallholder farmers, processors, traders, and transport workers. **Historical Foundation of Colombia**

Colombia, officially known as the Republic of Colombia, traces its modern founding to the period of independence from Spanish colonial rule. The movement for independence began in the early 19th century amid broader upheavals throughout Latin America.

On July 20, 1810, patriotic leaders in Bogotá declared the sovereignty of the territory that would become Colombia, marking the start of the struggle for full independence. This event is often considered the country's foundational moment. However, the process of achieving complete independence was protracted, involving years of conflict and political transformation.

A significant milestone came on July 7, 1819, when Simón Bolívar led forces to victory at the Battle of Boyacá, resulting in the liberation of much of New Granada (modern-day Colombia) from Spanish rule. While this battle established de facto independence, the formal establishment of the Republic of Colombia occurred later with the Congress of Angostura and the subsequent creation of Gran Colombia in 1821, which united several South American territories.

Modern Colombia adopted its current constitution in 1886 after the dissolution of Gran Colombia, solidifying its status as an independent nation. Nonetheless, July 20, 1810, remains celebrated annually as Independence Day, commemorating the country's assertion of self-rule and laying the groundwork for subsequent developments.
