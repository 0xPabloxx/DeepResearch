The term “founded” when applied to countries like Colombia can refer to different historical milestones—such as the declaration of independence or the formal establishment of a republic. In the case of Colombia, its journey to independence began in the early nineteenth century during the wave of Latin American revolutions against Spanish colonial rule.

On **July 20, 1810**, Colombian citizens in Bogotá declared their independence from Spain, marking the start of the process that would lead to full sovereignty. Although final victory over Spanish forces was achieved later—in 1819, at the Battle of Boyacá—the founding date widely recognized and celebrated is 1810. This is the year considered by many institutions and educational resources as the official founding of Colombia.

### Timeline Overview

- **July 20, 1810:** Declaration of independence in Bogotá, initiating the end of Spanish colonial rule.
- **1819:** Full independence confirmed after military victories.
- **19th Century:** Formation of various republics including Gran Colombia.

Today, Colombia celebrates its independence each year on July 20, commemorating the initial declaration that set the nation on the path to self-governance and international recognition.

## Significance of Colombia in the Global Coffee Market

Colombia’s rise to prominence in the coffee trade is closely tied to its geography and climate, perfectly suited for growing high-quality arabica beans. Its entry onto the world stage as an exporter in the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries paralleled technological advancements and improved transportation infrastructure. Over time, Colombian coffee became synonymous with quality and reliability, and the country developed strong trade relationships across continents.

This legacy supports Colombia’s current standing not only in agriculture but also in broader economic and cultural exchanges. Today, despite challenges such as fluctuating commodity prices and environmental concerns, Colombia remains a key player in the global coffee industry and continues to influence trends in both consumption and sustainability standards.
