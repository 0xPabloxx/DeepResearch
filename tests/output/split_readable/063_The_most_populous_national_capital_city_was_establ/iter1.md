## Identification of the Most Populous National Capital

Multiple reputable sources and urban population rankings consistently place Tokyo at the top among all national capitals. Several factors influence this ranking:

- **Metropolitan Area Size:** Tokyo’s Greater Tokyo Area houses approximately 37 million people, making it the most extensive capital metro region globally.
- **City Proper Population:** Even when considering only the city proper, Tokyo remains among the largest, though this number decreases compared to the wider metropolitan figure.
- **Alternative Capitals:** While cities like Delhi (India), Beijing (China), and others have large populations, none surpass Tokyo’s metropolitan size.

It is worth noting that different definitions of "capital" (such as administrative boundaries versus urban sprawl) can affect rankings, but overall, Tokyo is universally acknowledged as the most populous capital city.

## Historical Foundation of Tokyo

Tokyo, originally known as Edo, holds a rich history that predates its elevation as the Japanese capital. The concept of "establishment" can be interpreted in two major ways:

1. **Foundation of the City:**  
   Tokyo traces its origins to the settlement of Edo, which was formally founded in 1457 by Ōta Dōkan. He constructed Edo Castle during this time, marking the emergence of the area as an important regional center.

2. **Designation as National Capital:**  
   The city became the seat of government after the Meiji Restoration in 1868. At this point, Emperor Meiji moved the capital from Kyoto to Edo, which was renamed Tokyo ("Eastern Capital"). This event solidified Tokyo's status as Japan's political center.

Given the phrasing of the question—specifically "was established"—the foundational date of 1457 is generally regarded as the year Tokyo was first established as a city. The transformation into the imperial capital occurred much later, in 1868, but the original founding is reflected in historical records and references used in global city rankings.

## Global Perspective on Capital Cities

Other notable national capitals include Delhi and Beijing, both with enormous metropolitan populations as well. However, Tokyo’s unique combination of ancient roots and rapid modernization makes it distinct. Its continuous expansion and role as Japan’s economic, cultural, and political heart further reinforce its significance.

The term "establishment" for cities is typically linked to their earliest recorded foundation dates, rather than subsequent changes in political status. Therefore, although Tokyo's rise to capital status in 1868 was a critical moment, its establishment dates back to 1457.

## Conclusion

Based on historical records and current global rankings, the most populous national capital city—Tokyo, Japan—was established in the year **1457**. This marks the formal founding of Edo, which later evolved into Tokyo, and set the stage for its eventual role as the capital of Japan starting in 1868. Both dates are significant: 1457 as the foundation of the city itself, and 1868 as the year it was officially made the national capital.
