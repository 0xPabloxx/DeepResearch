# Question

# Ground Truth Answer

# Model Prediction


The longest Chinese dynasty is widely recognized as the Zhou Dynasty, which endured from approximately 1046 BCE until 256 BCE, spanning roughly 790 years. This remarkable longevity distinguishes the Zhou Dynasty from other major Chinese dynasties, such as the Han, Tang, Song, Ming, and Qing, each of which lasted several hundred years but fell short of the Zhou’s duration.

The founder and first king of the Zhou Dynasty was King Wu, whose personal name was Ji Fa. King Wu played a pivotal role in Chinese history by leading the coalition of tribes that defeated the last ruler of the preceding Shang Dynasty at the Battle of Muye. This victory marked the beginning of Zhou rule, ushering in what would become the longest-lasting era in ancient China.

Before ascending to kingship, King Wu’s father, King Wen, had begun the process of rallying opposition against the corrupt rule of the Shang. However, King Wen died before completing the conquest; thus, it was his son, King Wu, who took up the mantle and successfully led the Zhou forces to overthrow the Shang. Upon establishing his reign, King Wu solidified his authority and declared himself the new "Son of Heaven," a concept that would persist throughout imperial China.

The establishment of the Zhou Dynasty was not only significant for its duration but also for its profound impact on Chinese culture, governance, and philosophy. The Zhou introduced the "Mandate of Heaven," a doctrine that legitimized royal rule only when the emperor governed justly and failed otherwise—a principle that influenced centuries of Chinese political thought and justified dynastic transitions.

Under King Wu, the Zhou began a system of feudal governance, dividing the kingdom among loyal relatives and allies, each responsible for local administration. This structure provided stability and allowed for effective control over vast territories, contributing to the dynasty’s resilience.

While King Wen was highly esteemed and later given the title of "King" in recognition of his contributions, it was King Wu who served as the inaugural monarch of the Zhou Dynasty and set the policies that shaped its future. His decisive actions following the fall of the Shang laid the foundation for an era characterized by military innovation, bureaucratic development, and advances in literature, music, and philosophy.

In summary, the answer to the question “Who was the first king of the longest Chinese dynasty?” is:

**King Wu (Ji Fa) of the Zhou Dynasty**

He is celebrated both for his role as the founder of this enduring dynasty and for initiating traditions and philosophies that profoundly influenced the course of Chinese civilization.
