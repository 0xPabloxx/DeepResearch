## Background: San Jose State University’s History

San Jose State University traces its roots back to the mid-19th century when California was still transitioning from Mexican rule to American governance. Initially founded as the State Normal School of California in 1857, its mission was to prepare teachers for the rapidly growing communities in California. Over time, SJSU expanded its academic offerings beyond teacher training to include a broad spectrum of disciplines—engineering, business, sciences, and the arts. Today, it stands as a comprehensive urban university committed to providing accessible and transformative education to diverse student populations.

## What Is the Motto?

The official motto of San Jose State University is “Scientia et Labor,” which translates from Latin to “Science and Labor” or “Knowledge and Work.” This motto reflects the university’s longstanding commitment to excellence in both intellectual pursuit and practical application. 

The use of “Scientia et Labor” aligns with SJSU’s historical emphasis on integrating theoretical learning with real-world experience, innovation, and service. Since its inception, the university has maintained a focus on preparing students not only for scholarly achievement but also for productive engagement in society and industry.

### Meaning and Significance

- **Scientia** (Science/Knowledge): Represents the value placed on rigorous academic inquiry, research, and the advancement of knowledge across disciplines. SJSU encourages students to become critical thinkers and skilled professionals through evidence-based learning and discovery.
- **Labor** (Labor/Work): Symbolizes the dedication to practical skills, professional development, and community impact. It underscores the university’s tradition of linking classroom learning with hands-on experience, vocational training, and service to others.

Together, these elements encapsulate SJSU’s mission to equip graduates who are intellectually adept, industrious, and prepared to contribute meaningfully to their fields and communities.

It is important to distinguish between mottos and institutional slogans. For example, SJSU may use marketing phrases or mission statements like “Excellence in Action” or “Rigorous in requirements, meticulous in work,” but these are not formal mottos and serve different purposes.
