The tallest lattice tower in the world is widely recognized as the Ostankino Tower in Moscow, Russia. A lattice tower is typically defined as a structure made of a network of steel beams or columns arranged in a crisscross pattern, providing both stability and strength with relatively light materials. The Ostankino Tower stands out among these structures due to its record height and iconic design.

## Completion Year of the Tallest Lattice Tower

The Ostankino Tower was completed in 1967. This marked a significant milestone in engineering and architectural history during the mid-20th century, a time when ambitious projects were undertaken to demonstrate technological prowess and national pride.

### Background and Construction

Construction of the Ostankino Tower began in 1963, initiated under the leadership of Soviet officials eager to construct a landmark that would showcase the country’s technical capabilities. After four years of intensive work, the tower was officially opened on March 11, 1967.

The tower is renowned for its distinctive lattice structure—a mixture of concrete foundations, a steel framework, and an antenna spire. Its main support consists of a reinforced concrete shaft with an openwork steel framework above it, resulting in both aesthetic appeal and structural efficiency.

### Technical Specifications

Ostankino Tower holds several records:

| Specification        | Value           |
|----------------------|----------------|
| Height               | 540.1 meters   |
| Year Completed       | 1967           |
| Location             | Moscow, Russia |

This height makes Ostankino Tower the tallest free-standing structure in Europe, and it remains the tallest lattice tower globally.

### Significance and Impact

The completion of Ostankino Tower represented more than just an engineering achievement; it symbolized the Soviet Union’s aspirations during the Cold War era. The tower serves multiple purposes: broadcasting television and radio signals, observation platform for tourists, and even housing research facilities.

Its lattice design not only enables it to withstand harsh weather conditions but also offers opportunities for artistic and cultural expression. The tower has become an important part of Moscow’s cityscape and is frequently featured in media portrayals of Russia.

### Alternative Structures

While there are other tall lattice towers around the world—such as Tokyo Tower (Japan) and CN Tower (Canada)—these do not surpass Ostankino in height or maintain the same classification as a lattice tower. Some guyed masts exist that are taller, such as the KVLY-TV mast in the United States, but these are considered masts rather than lattice towers because they depend on external supports (guy wires). Thus, they are not generally included in the category of lattice towers in most technical references.

## Conclusion

In summary, the tallest lattice tower in the world—the Ostankino Tower in Moscow—was completed in 1967. Its remarkable height, innovative design, and historical importance have ensured its place as a global engineering icon and a testament to advancements in structural engineering of the 20th century.
