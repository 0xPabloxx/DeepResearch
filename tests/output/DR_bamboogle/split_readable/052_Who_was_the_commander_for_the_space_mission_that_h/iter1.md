# Question

Who was the commander for the space mission that had the first spacewalk?

# Ground Truth Answer

Pavel Belyayev

# Model Prediction


The commander for the space mission that had the first spacewalk was Pavel Belyayev. He led the Soviet Voskhod 2 mission, which launched on March 18, 1965. On this historic mission, Alexei Leonov became the first human to conduct an extravehicular activity (EVA), commonly known as a spacewalk. This pioneering event marked a major milestone in human space exploration and significantly advanced international competition in the space race between the Soviet Union and the United States.

## Background of the First Spacewalk

During the early years of human spaceflight, both the Soviet Union and the United States were striving to achieve significant milestones. While the Soviets had already sent the first man into space (Yuri Gagarin) and completed several orbital flights, the Americans focused on other technical advancements, such as rendezvous and docking procedures. A crucial goal emerged: performing an activity outside a spacecraft. The Soviet space program targeted this challenge by designing the Voskhod 2 mission specifically to include a spacewalk.

Alexei Leonov was selected as the cosmonaut who would perform the EVA, while Pavel Belyayev was chosen to serve as the commander of the mission. Their teamwork proved essential to the success and survival of the operation. Without Belyayev’s skilled piloting, Leonov’s risky exit from the capsule may not have been safely completed.

## Mission Details and Challenges

The Voskhod 2 mission was notable for its innovation and technical risks. The Voskhod 2 spacecraft was specially modified to accommodate two cosmonauts and featured a hatch designed to open outward for the EVA. Leonov exited the capsule using a spacesuit that was newly developed to protect him from the harsh vacuum of space.

However, the EVA did not go smoothly. Leonov encountered multiple hazards:
- His spacesuit inflated in the near-vacuum, making movement difficult and straining his ability to re-enter the capsule.
- Extreme temperatures outside the spacecraft threatened suit integrity.
- Communication issues added stress to the already complex procedure.

Through quick thinking and adaptation, both Leonov and Belyayev overcame these obstacles. After a tense period during re-entry, Leonov safely returned to the Voskhod 2 capsule, and the spacecraft landed successfully back on Earth.

## Key Figures in the Mission

### Pavel Belyayev: Mission Commander
Pavel Romanovich Belyayev was a veteran cosmonaut and military pilot. He underwent rigorous training to become one of the USSR’s earliest cosmonaut corps members. For Voskhod 2, he was responsible for piloting the spacecraft, ensuring the safety of himself and Leonov, and managing all phases of the mission, including launch, orbital maneuvers, and re-entry.

Belyayev had previously served as a fighter pilot in World War II and went on to have a distinguished career in the Soviet Air Force and later in civilian scientific organizations. His leadership and composure under pressure were critical to the mission’s success.

### Alexei Leonov: First Spacewalker
Alexei Arkhipovich Leonov was tasked with performing the EVA. His achievement made global headlines and cemented both his and Belyayev’s places in history. Leonov was known for his willingness to take risks and push boundaries in pursuit of scientific advancement.

## Significance of the First Spacewalk

The successful completion of the EVA by Leonov, under Belyayev’s command, demonstrated that humans could function outside their spacecraft in the hostile conditions of space. This accomplishment paved the way for future missions that required astronauts or cosmonauts to work outside their vehicles, such as constructing space stations, repairing satellites, and conducting scientific experiments.

For the United States, the first American spacewalk came just a few months later, on June 3, 1965, during the NASA Gemini 4 mission, which was commanded by James McDivitt and included astronaut Ed White. While this mission was historically important for the U.S., the world remembers the Soviet Voskhod 2 mission as the first overall spacewalk.

## Legacy

The Voskhod 2 mission and its participants inspired generations of scientists, engineers, and explorers. It set new standards for what was possible in human spaceflight and challenged both superpowers to continue advancing technology and safety protocols.

Today, EVAs are routine activities on projects like the International Space Station, and the skills first demonstrated by Leonov and Belyayev are essential to modern space operations.
