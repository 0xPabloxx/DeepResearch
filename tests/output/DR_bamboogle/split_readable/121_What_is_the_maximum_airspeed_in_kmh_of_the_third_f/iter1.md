## The Fastest Birds: Definitions and Categories

In the context of avian speeds, several categories are commonly referenced:

- **Level Flight Speed**: The maximum sustainable speed achieved during normal flying conditions, without diving or gliding. This is typically lower than diving speeds.
- **Diving (Stoop) Speed**: The velocity attained by birds during a steep, rapid dive, often used to capture prey from above. These speeds can exceed those of level flight by a large margin.
- **Gliding or Soaring Speed**: Speeds reached during sustained, wind-driven flight with minimal flapping, common among large birds like albatrosses and vultures.

Most authoritative lists, including the famous Wikipedia page “List of birds by speed,” distinguish between these categories and rank birds accordingly.

Golden eagles are also formidable in their own right, especially during dives. While not as fast as the peregrine falcon, golden eagles have been recorded reaching approximately 320 km/h (about 200 mph) when they plummet from high altitudes to attack prey on the ground.

### 3. White-backed Vulture (Gyps africanus)
As the third fastest bird, the white-backed vulture ranks just behind the two eagle species. This African scavenger is capable of diving at speeds up to 241 km/h (about 150 mph). Such high speeds are essential for seizing food quickly and defending carcasses against competitors in the harsh savannah environments where these vultures thrive.

## Why Do Diving Raptors Achieve Such High Speeds?

Birds like the peregrine falcon, golden eagle, and white-backed vulture rely on their aerodynamic shapes, specialized wing morphology, and powerful muscles for high-speed dives. During a stoop, they reduce cross-sectional area and streamline their body to minimize air resistance. Additionally, gravity assists in accelerating them rapidly before deploying their wings for recovery or maneuvering near the end of the dive.
