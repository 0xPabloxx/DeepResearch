The world’s tallest Ferris wheel is currently the Ain Dubai, located on Bluewaters Island in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. This record-breaking structure stands at a remarkable height of 250 meters (820 feet), making it the highest observation wheel ever built.

## Completion and Opening Timeline

The construction of Ain Dubai began in 2015, with significant milestones achieved over several years. After experiencing delays due to factors such as weather, logistical challenges, and a fire during construction, the project continued until the main structure was fully assembled in mid-2022. However, finishing touches—including mechanical, electrical systems, passenger cabins, and thorough safety inspections—took additional time.

Ain Dubai officially opened to the public on October 17, 2023. This marked both the completion of its construction and the start of regular operations for visitors. Prior to the official opening, the wheel underwent extensive testing and had limited trial runs towards the end of 2023, ensuring optimal functionality and safety standards before welcoming the general public.

## Significance and Technical Highlights

Ain Dubai quickly garnered international attention due to several key features:

- **Height:** At 250 meters, it surpasses all previous record-holders.
- **Number of Cabins:** The wheel is designed with 46 climate-controlled, glass-walled cabins, each capable of holding up to 28 passengers.
- **Construction Materials:** The majority of its structure consists of steel, engineered to withstand extreme temperatures and wind loads common in Dubai.
- **Energy Efficiency:** The wheel incorporates energy-saving technology, including LED lighting and regenerative braking systems.

## Comparison with Previous Record-Holders

Throughout history, various Ferris wheels have held the title of the world’s tallest. Here’s a brief overview of major record-breakers and their respective completion dates:

| Singapore Flyer    | Singapore         | 165             | 2008          |
| High Roller        | Las Vegas, USA    | 167.6           | 2014          |
| Ain Dubai          | Dubai, UAE        | 250             | 2023          |

*Note: The Star of London was an earlier name for the London Eye in planning stages; actual completion and opening occurred in 2000.

## Key Dates in the History of Ain Dubai

- **Construction Began:** 2015
- **Main Structure Assembly Completed:** Mid-2022
- **Official Public Opening:** October 17, 2023

While construction milestones, such as the assembly of the giant axle and rim, were reached earlier, the final completion aligns with the official opening to the public in October 2023.

The wheel also represents advancements in sustainable design and public experience, featuring panoramic views, entertainment options, and accessibility features that cater to diverse visitor groups.
