## Discovery of Plutonium

Plutonium (Pu), an artificial radioactive element, was first synthesized in December 1940 by researchers at the University of California, Berkeley. This historic achievement came through bombarding uranium with deuterons using the cyclotron machine. The team involved in this groundbreaking work included Glenn T. Seaborg, Edwin McMillan, Joseph W. Kennedy, and Arthur Wahl. Their collective efforts led to the identification of element 94, which was named after Pluto—the dwarf planet, following the naming tradition set by uranium and neptunium.

The laboratory where this discovery occurred was known as the Radiation Laboratory, now called Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. This institution was central to nuclear physics research during the early 20th century and played a pivotal role in atomic science advancements.

## Founding Date of the University

The University of California, Berkeley was formally established as part of the state’s commitment to higher education and public service. The founding date of the university is considered to be March 23, 1868. On this day, the state legislature passed the Organic Act, creating the University of California system and designating the location of its first campus in Berkeley.

Following the passage of the Organic Act, the actual opening of the Berkeley campus occurred later in 1868. Classes officially began on September 23, 1868, when the first cohort of students arrived at the newly founded university.
