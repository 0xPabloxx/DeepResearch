The protein content in egg yolks is an important consideration for those monitoring their dietary protein intake, whether for health, athletic performance, or specific nutritional goals. While most people associate eggs with protein primarily in the egg white, the yolk also provides a meaningful amount of protein and other essential nutrients.

When you boil an egg, the egg white and yolk undergo minor physical changes due to heat, but the overall nutrient composition remains largely unchanged. However, it’s important to note that cooking methods and egg size can influence the final protein content in terms of both absolute weight and concentration.

## Protein Content in a Single Boiled Egg Yolk

On average, a large egg yolk weighs about 17–18 grams. According to widely recognized nutrition databases, the protein content per 100 grams of egg yolk is approximately 16.4–16.5 grams. Therefore, the protein in a single large yolk works out to around 2.8–2.9 grams.

For smaller eggs, such as medium or jumbo, the protein content will vary slightly, but 2.8 grams is a reliable estimate for a large yolk commonly found in grocery stores.

## Protein in Four Boiled Egg Yolks

If you consume four boiled egg yolks, you can expect the following:

So, eating four boiled egg yolks provides approximately **11–12 grams of protein**. This figure assumes the use of large yolks; if your eggs are significantly larger or smaller, adjust the calculations accordingly.

## Factors Affecting Protein Content

While the basic calculation above gives a good estimate, several factors can cause slight variations in the protein content of egg yolks:

1. **Egg Size**: As mentioned, the size of the egg determines the weight of the yolk. Jumbo eggs will have bigger yolks and thus slightly more protein, whereas small or medium eggs will have less.

2. **Cooking Method**: Boiling causes some moisture loss, which can slightly concentrate nutrients, including protein. However, the difference is minimal and usually negligible for practical purposes.

3. **Diet of the Hen**: The hen’s diet can impact the nutritional profile of the egg yolk, including its protein levels, though these effects are typically modest.

## Nutritional Context

Protein is one of the macronutrients vital for building and repairing tissues, synthesizing enzymes and hormones, and supporting immune function. The recommended daily intake for an average adult is about 50 grams of protein, although requirements may vary based on age, sex, and activity level.

To put the protein content of four boiled egg yolks into perspective:
- It provides approximately 22–23% of the daily protein requirement for an average adult consuming 50 grams of protein per day.
- It also contributes additional nutrients such as choline, vitamins A, D, E, and K, and various minerals like iron and selenium—many of which are concentrated in the yolk.

## Summary

In summary, **four boiled egg yolks contain about 11–12 grams of protein**, assuming large yolks are used. This makes egg yolks a valuable source of protein, especially for those incorporating eggs into their diet for diverse nutrients. If you're tracking protein intake closely, you can adjust this value slightly based on the actual size of your eggs or the specific type of egg product you use.
