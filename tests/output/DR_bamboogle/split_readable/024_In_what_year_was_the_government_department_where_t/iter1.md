**Background of the Internet’s Origin**

The technology that evolved into the Internet started with ARPANET (Advanced Research Projects Agency Network), a pioneering computer networking project. ARPANET was developed to enable researchers and military personnel to share information across vast distances. This development was motivated by strategic interests in communications reliability, including resilience against attacks, and the desire to foster collaborative scientific research.

ARPANET itself was managed by a department within the U.S. government. Although commonly referred to as an “agency,” the funding and oversight of ARPANET were carried out by a specialized branch of the Department of Defense. Specifically, this was the Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA), which later became known as the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA). 

**Understanding the Term “Government Department”**

In everyday language, especially in historical and educational discussions about the Internet’s beginnings, the term “government department” most frequently refers to large federal entities such as the U.S. Department of Defense. While ARPA/DARPA technically falls under the umbrella of the Department of Defense, it functions more as an agency tasked with advancing high-tech research. Nevertheless, in common parlance and in many historical accounts, ARPANET and, by extension, the Internet, are described as originating under the auspices of the Department of Defense.

**Founding Year of the Relevant Government Entity**

The question asks specifically about the year the relevant government department was founded. In this context, the key department responsible for initiating ARPANET—and therefore considered the birthplace of the Internet—is the United States Department of Defense.

The Department of Defense was officially established in 1947. Its foundation stemmed from the National Security Act, which came into effect on September 18, 1947. This act reorganized the American armed forces and defense structures after World War II, unifying the previously separate Army and Navy Departments under one consolidated entity—the Department of Defense.

**Clarification on Other Entities**

It’s important to distinguish between the Department of Defense and its research arm, ARPA/DARPA:
- **Department of Defense (DoD):** Established in 1947. Responsible for coordinating all activities relating to national defense and had ultimate authority over projects like ARPANET.
- **Advanced Research Projects Agency (ARPA):** Created in 1958. Operated within the DoD and directly managed the initial research projects that led to ARPANET.

While ARPA was the agency that specifically managed ARPANET and thus played a central role in technical developments leading to the Internet, the Department of Defense was the overarching government department that provided funding, oversight, and strategic direction.

**Conclusion**

In summary, the Department of Defense was founded in 1947. It oversaw the creation of ARPANET via its research agency ARPA (created in 1958), making it the official government department associated with the origin of the Internet.
