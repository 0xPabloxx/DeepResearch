## Background on the "Quit India" Speech

On August 8, 1942, at the Bombay session of the All India Congress Committee, Mahatma Gandhi delivered his iconic "Quit India" speech. This speech was a call to action for Indians to fight for complete independence using peaceful means but with determination and urgency. The movement that followed sought immediate self-rule and was characterized by widespread protests and demonstrations across India. Gandhi’s leadership in this movement solidified his status as a central figure in India’s freedom struggle.

## Birth Details of Mahatma Gandhi

Mahatma Gandhi was born on **October 2, 1869**, in Porbandar, a coastal town in present-day Gujarat, India. His early life was influenced by his family's values, religious background, and the social environment of western India. He later studied law in London and practiced law in South Africa before returning to India to lead the independence movement.

## Significance of the "Quit India" Speech

Gandhi’s “Quit India” speech became one of the most powerful calls for freedom and resistance in modern history. In his address, he famously urged Indians to make the final push for self-governance, stating, “Now, I do not want our countrymen to misinterpret this call as a call to violence. We have adopted Satyagraha, and it will be our guide until the end.” His use of nonviolence resonated globally, making him an international symbol of peace and justice.

The movement led to mass arrests of leaders, including Gandhi, and widespread civil unrest. Despite brutal suppression by British authorities, the spirit of the Quit India Movement inspired millions to continue fighting for freedom.

## Legacy

Mahatma Gandhi’s birthdate, October 2, 1869, is now celebrated annually in India as Gandhi Jayanti, a national holiday honoring his contributions to peace and independence. His philosophy and methods of nonviolent resistance have influenced countless movements around the world, including the American civil rights movement and anti-apartheid struggles.

In summary, the individual who delivered the "Quit India" speech was Mahatma Gandhi, born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, India. His life and work remain integral to the historical narrative of both India and global movements for justice and equality.
